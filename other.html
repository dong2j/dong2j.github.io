1. Who first suggested that a few oceans of water is tied up inside Earth?

I feel a thousand capacities spring up in me. I am arch, gay, languid, melancholy by turns. I am rooted, but I flow.

—VIRGINIA WOOLF, THE WAVES

About all the other human tears that have soaked the whole earth through, from crust to core, I don't say a word, I've purposely narrowed down my theme.

—FYODOR DOSTOEVSKY, THE BROTHERS KARAMAZOV (TRANS. RICHARD PEVEAR & LARISSA VOLOKHONSKY)

2. What we can never, ever know?

The mushroom of a morning does not know (what takes place between) the beginning and end of a month; the short-lived cicada does not know (what takes place between) the spring and autumn. These are instances of a short term of life.

—ZHUANG ZHOU, ENJOYMENT IN UNTROUBLED EASE (TRANS. JAMES LEGGE)

All experience seems to be of this character; We never have perfectly clean-cut knowledge of anything, but all our experience is surrounded by a twilight zone, a penumbra of uncertainty, into which we have not yet penetrated. This penumbra is as truly an unexplored region as any other region beyond experiment.

—PERCY BRIDGMAN, THE LOGIC OF MODERN PHYSICS

3.Who presented the strongest case yet for human-caused climate change?

The Earth has a skin, and this skin has diseases. One of these diseases is called, for example, ‘humanity’.

—FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE, THUS SPOKE ZARATHUSTRA (TRANS. GRAHAM PARKES)
