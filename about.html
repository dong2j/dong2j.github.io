I am a Ph.D. candidate in Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences at Harvard University working with Prof. Rebecca Fischer.

I study the physical properties and chemical evolution of terrestrial planetary interiors, using a combination of high-pressure experiments and geochemical models.

I am especially interested in the origin and fate of volatiles as well as early planetary differentiation processes, on both the Earth and other inner planets of the Solar System.

I received my bachelor's degree in Earth and Environmental Sciences from University of Michigan in 2017.
