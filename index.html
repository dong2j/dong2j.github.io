Research

My research seeks to understand the large-scale structure and long-term evolution of Earth and other planets by investigating how their constituent materials behave at the atomic level.

My group combines laboratory experiments with computer simulations and uses a variety of tools, including multi-anvil presses, diamond anvil cells, molecular dynamics simulations, thermodynamic modeling, and machine learning. These methods allow us to connect the physics and chemistry of planetary materials to geophysical, geochemical, and astronomical observations.

Current topics of interest include:

  • Material properties under extreme conditions

  • Water in terrestrial planet evolution and habitability

  • Structure and evolution of giant planets and exoplanets
